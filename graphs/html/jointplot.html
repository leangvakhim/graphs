What is a Joint Plot? 🕵️‍♂️

In Data Analysis (EDA), we often want to see how two things relate to each other. A Joint Plot is amazing because it combines three graphs into one. It shows the relationship between two variables, while simultaneously showing how each individual variable is distributed.

📊

1. Top Graph (The Blue Bars)

This is a Histogram for the X-axis (Hours Studied). It ignores the scores entirely and just tells you: "How long do most students study?" You can easily see the most common study times by looking at the tallest blue bars.

📈

2. Right Graph (The Green Bars)

This is the Histogram for the Y-axis (Exam Scores). It ignores the study hours and just tells you: "What were the most common exam scores?" It helps you spot if scores are mostly high, low, or average.

✨

3. Center Graph (The Pink Dots)

This is a Scatter Plot. This is where the magic happens! Every dot is one student. By looking at the pattern of the dots, you can answer: "Do students who study more get higher scores?" (In our chart, yes—the dots form an upward slope!).
