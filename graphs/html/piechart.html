Demystifying the Pie Chart

In Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), we use pie charts to see how a dataset is divided into different categories. Think of it like slicing up a delicious pizza!

Example: Customer Subscription Types

Hover over or tap the colorful slices to see exact details!

Key Features Simplified

The "Whole" Pie (100%)

The entire circle represents all of your data (100%). In our example, it represents every single customer combined. If you add up all the slices, they will always equal the total amount.

The Slices (Categories)

Each slice is a different group or category. The bigger the slice, the more data it holds. A quick glance tells you that "Basic Plan" is the largest group without even looking at the numbers!

Colors & Legends

Different colors separate the groups so your eyes don't get confused. The "Legend" (usually at the top or bottom) acts like a map key, telling you exactly which color belongs to which group.

When to use it in EDA?

Use pie charts when you want to show proportions (percentages) and you only have a few categories (ideally 2 to 5). If you have 20 categories, the slices become too tiny to read!
