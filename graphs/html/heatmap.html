Key Heatmap Features

1.0

The Diagonal Line

Notice the dark blue line going from top-left to bottom-right? These are always exactly 1.0 because it's comparing a variable against itself (e.g., Study Hours vs Study Hours). It's a perfect match!

-ve

Red Colors (Negative)

Red means variables move in opposite directions. If one goes up, the other goes down. Example: More Video Games usually means Lower Test Scores.

+ve

Blue Colors (Positive)

Blue means variables move in the same direction. If one goes up, the other goes up. Example: More Study Hours usually means Higher Test Scores.

0.0

White/Faint Colors (Neutral)

White or very pale colors mean the value is near zero. There is no real relationship between the two variables.
