The Magic of Line Plots in EDA

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is like being a detective with data. A Line Plot is your best tool for seeing how things change over time. Let's break it down!

Anatomy of a Line Plot

1

The Timeline (X-Axis)

Look at the bottom horizontal line (Jan, Feb, Mar...). In EDA, the X-axis almost always represents time or a sequence. It reads from left to right, showing us the journey from the past to the future.

2

The Measurements (Y-Axis)

Look at the vertical line on the left (0 to 100). This shows what we are measuring. In this case, it's the number of items sold. The higher up you go, the bigger the number!

3

The Story (Lines & Dots)

Every dot is a single piece of data (e.g., exactly how many Ice Creams were sold in July).

The line connecting them reveals the "Trend". Instead of staring at a boring spreadsheet of numbers, the line instantly tells our brain: "Ice cream sales go way up in the summer!"

4

The Key (Legend)

Look at the top of the chart. The Legend acts like a map key. It tells us that the Pink Line is Ice Cream and the Orange Line is Hot Chocolate. In EDA, this helps us compare multiple things at the exact same time.

5

Patterns & Crossovers

Notice where the lines cross over in April and October? In EDA, this is a "Eureka!" moment. It shows exactly when consumer habits flip from cold drinks to hot drinks due to changing seasons.
