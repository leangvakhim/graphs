Understanding Scatterplots 📊

A scatterplot is simply a graph that plots dots to show the relationship between two different things. In Data Analysis (EDA), we look at these dots to find hidden stories. Let's learn how to read them!

Pattern 1

Positive Correlation

"They grow up together! 📈"

When the dots slope upwards from left to right, it means that as one thing increases, the other thing also increases.

Real-world example: Hours spent studying (bottom) vs. Test score (side). The more you study, the higher your score tends to be.

Pattern 2

Negative Correlation

"A balancing act! 📉"

When the dots slope downwards from left to right, it means that as one thing increases, the other thing decreases. They move in opposite directions.

Real-world example: Hours spent playing video games (bottom) vs. Test score (side). More games might mean lower scores.

Pattern 3

No Correlation

"Totally random! 🤷"

When the dots look like a messy cloud with no clear direction, it means these two things have nothing to do with each other. Knowing one doesn't help you guess the other.

Real-world example: Your shoe size (bottom) vs. Your math test score (side). One has no impact on the other!

Feature 4

Outliers

"The odd ones out! 👽"

Most dots follow a rule, but sometimes a dot sits far away from the rest of the group. We call these "Outliers". In EDA, we investigate these because they might be a mistake, or something very special!

Real-world example: A neighborhood where most houses cost $300k, but there is one giant mansion that costs $5 Million. That mansion is an outlier.

Feature 5

Clusters

"Birds of a feather! 🐦"

Sometimes dots bunch together in distinct groups or islands. This tells us that our data has different types of categories hiding inside it!

Real-world example: Height vs Weight of animals. You might see one cluster for "Dogs" and a totally separate cluster for "Horses".
